# Pay-by-social

Pay-by-social is LitZap's signature capability: **send money to someone by their X, Discord, or email — even if they've never used LitZap or crypto.** The funds are held safely on-chain and released only to the verified owner of that identity, or returned to the sender if unclaimed.

This is powered by the **LitZapEscrow** contract ([`0x315F…fE30`](https://liteforge.hub.caldera.xyz/address/0x315Fd6effBd3aDbd1CfBc53BE5d6CFB32A23fE30)).

## The flow

<Steps>

### Sender pays a handle

You enter, say, `x:flamingo` and an amount. LitZap computes a **recipient key** — a hash of the identity, `keccak256("x:flamingo")` — so the on-chain record reveals nothing about who the recipient is beyond that hash.

### Funds lock in escrow

The amount (USDC or zkLTC) is locked in `LitZapEscrow` against that recipient key, with an **expiry** (e.g. 7 days). The sender gets a shareable claim link to pass along if they want.

### Recipient shows up

The recipient signs in to LitZap with that same X / Discord / email — which quietly creates their wallet — and a **"Money waiting for you"** prompt appears.

### Ownership is verified

LitZap's verification signer confirms, through the recipient's own social login, that they truly own the handle. It then signs a release **bound to the recipient's wallet address**.

### Recipient claims

The recipient submits the claim with that signature. The contract checks the signature against its trusted signer and pays out to the verified address.

### Or it auto-refunds

If nobody claims before expiry, the sender calls `refund` and gets their money back. Funds are never stuck.

</Steps>

## Why it's safe

<Callout type="info">
  The release signature is over `(chainId, escrow, escrowId, recipientAddress)`. Because it's bound to the **recipient's address**, it cannot be replayed, redirected, or front-run by a bystander watching the chain.
</Callout>

- **No premature custody** — only the recipient who proves the handle can claim; until then funds sit in escrow.
- **No theft** — the signer authorizes a payout to one specific verified address only.
- **No stuck funds** — every escrow is refundable by the sender after expiry.
- **The signer can't rug** — it can only authorize releases to verified claimants, never to itself, and is rotatable by the contract owner.

## What the recipient experiences

For someone brand-new to crypto, the whole thing feels like: *"Someone sent me money on X → I signed in → I tapped Claim → it's in my balance."* The wallet creation, key management, and on-chain release all happen underneath. **Getting paid is the onboarding.**

## A composable primitive

`LitZapEscrow` is permissionless: any person, business, or other LitVM project can create escrows against an identity and rely on the same verified-release mechanism. See [Build on LitZap](/build-on-litzap).
