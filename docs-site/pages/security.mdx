# Security & trust

LitZap is built so that you trust the **contracts**, not the company. Here's exactly what is guaranteed by code, what relies on helper services, and the honest limits of each.

## Non-custodial by design

- Your wallet is **self-custodied** — you control the keys, created and bound to your login.
- **Completed payments never sit in a LitZap contract.** They pass straight from sender to recipient.
- The only funds ever held on-chain are **open escrows and claims**, and those are **always refundable by the sender** after expiry.

## What's enforced by the contracts

- **Fee ceiling.** The maximum protocol fee is hard-capped in code and cannot be raised beyond it. It is currently set to zero.
- **Reentrancy protection** on payment and escrow paths.
- **Pausability** for emergency response, owner-controlled.
- **Address-bound escrow releases.** A claim signature is valid only for one specific recipient address, preventing replay and front-running.

## The trust assumptions (stated plainly)

<Callout type="warning">
  We don't hide the parts that require trust. Here they are.
</Callout>

**The verification signer.** Pay-by-social relies on an off-chain signer that attests "this user owns this handle" and authorizes the matching release. Its power is strictly limited: it can **only** authorize a payout to a verified claimant's address — it cannot move funds to itself, drain escrows, or redirect a payment. It is rotatable by the contract owner. If it were ever offline, escrows still auto-refund to senders after expiry, so no funds are lost.

**Gas sponsorship.** A relayer tops up wallets with small amounts of gas to smooth onboarding. This is a convenience LitZap currently funds on testnet — not an unconditional guarantee. (This is why we never claim "no gas.")

**Operational keys.** The signer and relayer are operational keys held server-side and never exposed in the app. On testnet they are funded throwaway accounts. Hardening for mainnet (multisig / key management) is on the [Roadmap](/roadmap).

## Honest status

LitZap is in active development on **testnet**. The contracts are deployed and verifiable, the flows work end to end, and we are stabilising features ahead of a testnet launch. We have **not** completed a third-party security audit yet — an audit is a prerequisite before handling real value on mainnet, and it's on the roadmap. We'd rather state this clearly than imply a maturity we haven't earned.
